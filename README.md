# 📊 Digital Marketing Performance Analysis (Google vs. Facebook Ads)

## 📌 Project Overview
This project focuses on analyzing the performance of digital marketing campaigns across **Google Ads** and **Facebook Ads**. By examining key metrics such as spending, impressions, clicks, and efficiency indicators like CPC (Cost Per Click) and CPA (Cost Per Acquisition), I have extracted actionable insights to optimize budget allocation and campaign effectiveness.

## 🛠 Tools & Skills Used
* **Excel Pivot Tables:** For data aggregation, cleaning, and summarization.
* **Data Visualization:** Applied various charts (Line, Bar, Pie, and Stacked Bar) to identify trends and patterns.
* **KPI Analysis:** Evaluating performance through Cost Per Click (CPC) and Cost Per Acquisition (CPA).
* **Data-Driven Decision Making:** Formulating recommendations based on demographic and platform-specific data.

---

## 📈 Key Insights & Visualizations

### 1. Overall Channel Comparison
While Google Ads commands a higher total budget, Facebook Ads offers a more competitive entry point with lower average CPC.

> **Spending Trend:** Analysis of spending dynamics from late 2019 through mid-2020. Google Ads saw a significant peak in early 2020 compared to Facebook Ads.


> **Cost Analysis:** The data shows a downward trend in Google Ads CPC over time, while Facebook Ads maintained a lower, more stable cost per click.

---

### 2. Audience & Demographic Segmentation
I analyzed which age groups and devices yielded the highest return on investment.


> **Device Performance (Google):** Breakdown of performance by age groups (18-65+) and devices (Desktop, Mobile, Tablet). Identifying high-cost segments helps in refined targeting.


> **Age Distribution (Facebook):** The majority of the spend is concentrated in the 25-34 age group, indicating a younger core audience on this platform.

---

### 3. Campaign Strategy (Sub-channels)
Categorizing campaigns into **Brand**, **Generic**, and **Competitor** types to understand budget distribution.

> **Strategy:** Generic campaigns account for 52% of the total spend, focusing on broad reach and top-of-funnel awareness.

---

## 💡 Conclusions & Recommendations
* **Cost Efficiency:** Facebook Ads is more efficient for high-volume click acquisition at a lower cost.
* **Audience Focus:** The 25-34 age bracket is the most active across both platforms; increasing focus here could maximize engagement.
* **Optimization:** High CPC "Competitor" campaigns in Google Ads should be audited to ensure the conversion rate justifies the premium cost.
